Eligibility

Beta Alpha Psi is open to students who meet the following criteria:

  • Enrolled in accounting, finance, or information systems at Samford University
  • Completed Intermediate Accounting I
  • Maintain a minimum 3.0 overall GPA and 3.25 major GPA
  • Committed to attending chapter meetings and completing service hours

How Members Are Selected

There's no formal application. After students complete Intermediate Accounting I, our faculty advisor Mrs. Newberry reviews GPAs and extends personal invitations to qualifying students. If you meet the criteria, expect an email from her.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the time commitment?
Members are expected to attend chapter meetings and complete a minimum number of service and professional development hours each semester. Most members find the time commitment manageable alongside coursework — typically 2–4 hours per week during the semester.
Do I have to be on the CPA track?
No. BAP is open to accounting, finance, and information systems students. While many members pursue the CPA, others go into corporate finance, consulting, government, or graduate school. The chapter provides value regardless of your post-graduation path.
Are there dues or fees?
Yes. New members pay a one-time national induction fee, and there are modest chapter dues each semester to cover events and operations. Specific amounts are shared with invitees during onboarding.
When are members selected?
Mrs. Newberry reviews GPAs and extends invitations at the end of each semester, once Intermediate Accounting I grades are finalized. Most students join during their junior year.
Can I get involved before being inducted?
Many of our events — workshops, panels, and study groups — are open to all accounting and finance students. Watch the events section above and feel free to come to anything that interests you, even before you're officially a member.
